दाल भात Dal Bhat

A nourishing Nepali staple of lentils and rice, enriched with vegetables and aromatic spices. This DASH-friendly version reduces sodium while maintaining authentic flavors.

Servings
4 people
Prep Time
15 mins
Cook Time
30 mins

Ingredients

Dal (Lentils)

  • Yellow Lentils 1 cup
  • Tomato 1 medium, chopped
  • Spinach 2 cups, chopped
  • Onion 1 medium, diced
  • Garlic 4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger 1-inch piece, minced

Spices

  • Turmeric 1/2 teaspoon
  • Cumin Seeds 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander 1 teaspoon, ground
  • Olive Oil 2 tablespoons

Rice

  • Brown Rice 1 cup
  • Water 2 cups

Garnish

  • Cilantro 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Lemon 1, cut into wedges

Instructions

  1. 1.

    Rinse lentils and rice separately until water runs clear.

  2. 2.

    Cook brown rice with 2 cups water until tender, about 25 minutes.

  3. 3.

    In a pot, heat oil and add cumin seeds until they sizzle.

  4. 4.

    Add onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until onions are translucent.

  5. 5.

    Add turmeric, coriander, and tomatoes. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

  6. 6.

    Add lentils and 3 cups water. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 20 minutes.

  7. 7.

    Add spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes.

  8. 8.

    Serve hot dal over brown rice, garnished with cilantro and lemon wedges.

DASH Diet Notes

This recipe has been adapted to be DASH-compliant by:

  • Using brown rice instead of white rice for added fiber and nutrients
  • Adding spinach for extra minerals and vitamins
  • Using no added salt, relying on spices for flavor
  • Including heart-healthy olive oil instead of ghee
  • Adding extra vegetables for increased potassium content
  • Using lentils as a plant-based protein source rich in minerals
