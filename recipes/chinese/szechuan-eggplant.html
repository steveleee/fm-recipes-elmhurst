川味茄子鹰嘴豆 Spicy Szechuan Eggplant & Chickpeas

A vegetarian twist on the classic Szechuan eggplant dish, incorporating protein-rich chickpeas. This DASH-friendly version maintains the signature spicy flavor while keeping sodium low.

Servings
4 people
Prep Time
20 mins
Cook Time
15 mins

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • Chinese Eggplant 3 medium, cut into strips
  • Chickpeas 2 cans (15 oz each), drained and rinsed
  • Bell Peppers 2 medium, sliced

Aromatics & Spices

  • Garlic 6 cloves, minced
  • Ginger 2-inch piece, minced
  • Dried Chilies 4-6, deseeded and chopped
  • Szechuan Pepper 1 teaspoon, ground

Sauce

  • Rice Vinegar 2 tablespoons
  • Coconut Aminos 3 tablespoons (replaces soy sauce)
  • Vegetable Stock 1/2 cup, low-sodium
  • Cornstarch 1 tablespoon mixed with 2 tablespoons water

Instructions

  1. 1.

    Cut eggplant into strips and soak in salted water for 15 minutes. Drain and pat dry.

  2. 2.

    Mix sauce ingredients (except cornstarch slurry) in a bowl.

  3. 3.

    Heat a large non-stick wok over medium-high heat.

  4. 4.

    Dry-fry eggplant until softened and lightly browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside.

  5. 5.

    In the same wok, stir-fry garlic, ginger, dried chilies, and Szechuan pepper until fragrant.

  6. 6.

    Add bell peppers and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.

  7. 7.

    Add chickpeas and cook for 2 minutes.

  8. 8.

    Return eggplant to wok, add sauce mixture.

  9. 9.

    Stir in cornstarch slurry and cook until sauce thickens.

  10. 10.

    Serve hot with brown rice or whole grain noodles.

Nutrition Information

Calories
280
Protein
12g
Sodium
180mg
Potassium
820mg

DASH Diet Notes

This recipe has been specially adapted to be DASH-compliant by:

  • Using chickpeas as a plant-based protein source rich in fiber
  • Replacing traditional soy sauce with coconut aminos to reduce sodium
  • Adding bell peppers for extra vegetables and nutrients
  • Using dry-frying technique to reduce oil content
  • Incorporating low-sodium vegetable stock
  • Relying on spices and aromatics for flavor instead of salt
