花菜炒饭 Cauliflower Fried Rice

A low-carb twist on traditional fried rice using cauliflower rice, enriched with eggs and tofu. This DASH-friendly version is low in sodium but packed with flavor and nutrients.

Servings
4 people
Prep Time
15 mins
Cook Time
10 mins

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • Cauliflower 1 large head, riced (about 4 cups)
  • Firm Tofu 14 oz, cubed and pressed
  • Eggs 2 large, beaten

Vegetables

  • Carrots 2 medium, diced
  • Peas 1 cup frozen
  • Scallions 4, chopped
  • Garlic 4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger 1-inch piece, minced

Seasonings

  • Coconut Aminos 2 tablespoons (replaces soy sauce)
  • Rice Vinegar 1 tablespoon
  • Sesame Oil 2 teaspoons
  • White Pepper 1/4 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. 1.

    Rice cauliflower in a food processor until it resembles rice grains.

  2. 2.

    Press tofu for 15 minutes, then cut into small cubes.

  3. 3.

    Heat 1 teaspoon sesame oil in a large non-stick wok over medium-high heat.

  4. 4.

    Scramble eggs until just set, remove and set aside.

  5. 5.

    In the same wok, cook tofu until lightly browned. Remove and set aside.

  6. 6.

    Add remaining oil, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry until fragrant.

  7. 7.

    Add carrots and peas, cook for 2-3 minutes.

  8. 8.

    Add cauliflower rice, cook until tender-crisp (about 5 minutes).

  9. 9.

    Return eggs and tofu to wok, add coconut aminos and rice vinegar.

  10. 10.

    Season with white pepper and garnish with scallions.

Nutrition Information

Calories
220
Protein
15g
Sodium
185mg
Potassium
680mg

DASH Diet Notes

This recipe has been specially adapted to be DASH-compliant by:

  • Using cauliflower rice instead of white rice to reduce carbs and increase vegetables
  • Replacing soy sauce with coconut aminos to significantly reduce sodium
  • Including tofu and eggs for lean protein
  • Adding extra vegetables for increased potassium and fiber
  • Using healthy fats from sesame oil in moderation
  • Enhancing flavor with herbs and spices instead of salt
